Bio

I'm a former Anglican priest who left the church, became a university executive, and spent the next decade reading Nietzsche, the Tantric philosophers of medieval Kashmir, and anyone else who refused to separate the life of the mind from the life of the body.

My work sits at the intersection of Western philosophy and Trika Shaivism — the non-dual tradition that treats consciousness, not matter, as the ground of reality, and sees pleasure and engagement as paths to understanding rather than obstacles.

I'm interested in what happens when you stop trying to improve yourself and start paying attention instead. Most of what passes for wisdom is just fear wearing a toga. I'd rather be honest than enlightened.

Core Ideas

A few positions I keep returning to in my writing.

Nothing Is Missing
You are not a broken thing awaiting repair. The self-improvement industry profits from a lie about your insufficiency.
Consciousness First
Awareness is not a byproduct of matter. It is the ground. This changes everything about how we relate to experience.
The Body Knows
Pleasure, desire, and embodiment are not obstacles to wisdom. They are its instruments. The ascetic tradition got this backwards.
Philosophy Must Be Lived
Ideas that don't survive contact with Monday morning are decoration, not philosophy. Rigour means real-world application.
